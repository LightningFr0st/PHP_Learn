About Nordic nature

More than half the Nordic lands lie beneath a dense canopy of trees. You can walk from the southernmost county of Sweden, to Lapland, without once leaving the forest.

Northern Europe was covered by up to three kilometres of thick ice during the succession of ice ages. The fjords were carved by the power of those forces of nature.

The geography of Norway is dominated by vast mountain ranges broken up by valleys and fjords. Less than 10% of the country's area is arable, and the rest is mountainous.

The northern lights most commonly occur within the geographic area beneath the auroral oval. It encompasses all scandinavian countries.
